Course Information

Course Objective

This course will provide you with information about the Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act (AODA). The course also covers aspects of the Ontario Human Rights Code ("the Code") that pertain to people with disabilities. At the conclusion of the training course, you will be able to recognize your rights and responsibilities under the Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act (AODA) and the Ontario Human Rights Code. 

Course Modules

To help you achieve the course objective, the course will cover the following topics:

  • Introduction
  • Module 1 – Compliance Requirements
  • Module 2 – Information and Communications Standards
  • Module 3 – Employment Standards
  • Module 4 – Transportation Standards
  • Module 5 – Design of Public Spaces Standards
  • Module 6 – Customer Service Standards
  • Module 7 – The AODA and the Ontario Human Rights Code

Course Interactions

This course is presented in a responsive vertical layout. Use your device controls to scroll down to reveal content. When you encounter a Continue button, select it to proceed.To aid in the retention of the important information presented in this course, each module includes some interactive activities. Explore interactive elements with your device controls to reveal additional information. You can repeat the interactive activities as often as you'd like.

Module Quizzes

At the end of each module, you will complete a Module Quiz. The quiz questions provide you with feedback on your understanding of concepts presented in the module. You will have an unlimited number of attempts to answer the module quiz questions.

Final Test

After you complete the final module, you will be evaluated through a multiple-choice test. You must achieve 75% to pass the test. Upon successful completion of the test, a certificate will be emailed to you.
